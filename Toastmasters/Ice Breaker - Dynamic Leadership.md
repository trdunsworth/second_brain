When you access Basecamp, the icebreaker speech is meant to give members of the club a chance to get to know something about you. Since everyone in this room knows me pretty well already, it's a challenge for me to find something to talk about. Yes, I'm admitting that I might be boring underneath everything.

I am going to theme my icebreaker a little differently; I'm going to introduce you to why I chose this pathway as my next step in Toastmasters. I lost a promotion because the panel and the centre director both told me that I didn't have enough leadership skills, in their opinions, to succeed at the position.

Being honest, that felt a little like the parable of the prophet not being recognized in his hometown. 

Even though, after a few failed attempts to become an IT manager in a 9-1-1 centre, I recognize *THAT* pathway isn't for me, that doesn't mean that I shouldn't develop the leadership skills necessary for me to push forward into my communities. Maybe I can find a way to leverage the more quiet leadership style I thought I had developed over many years to find other opportunities to lead with data in 9-1-1.

I am looking forward to this pathway and exploring it to find my leadership style. I am certain that the clubs in the area where I am serving as Area Director will love it when I get a better handle on my style. Seriously, I have worked over the years to build a teaching style, but I've not really given any thought to a leadership style. In many cases, rather than leading, I've just done the tasks that needed to be done and "led" by doing.
