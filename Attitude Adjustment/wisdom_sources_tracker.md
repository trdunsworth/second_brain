### Philosophical Sources

#### Taoism
Taoism focuses on living in harmony with the Tao (the natural order), practicing Wu Wei (non-action/effortless action), balancing Yin-Yang, and cultivating the Three Treasures of compassion, frugality, and humility. Practice daily mindfulness, study foundational texts like "Tao Te Ching," and integrate these principles into decision-making and daily life.

#### Buddhism
Buddhism offers practical tools for personal development through the Four Noble Truths and Eightfold Path. Practice mindfulness meditation to cultivate awareness, develop compassion (Karuna), understand impermanence, and integrate these insights into emotional regulation and relationship building.

#### Stoicism
Stoicism teaches the Dichotomy of Control (focus on what we can control) and the Four Cardinal Virtues (wisdom, courage, temperance, justice). Practice daily resilience techniques, negative visualization, and integrate Stoic principles into decision-making and stress management.
### Psychological Sources

#### Dr. Thomas Moore - Soul Centered Psychology
Dr. Thomas Moore integrates depth psychology (Jungian and Archetypal Psychology) with spirituality, emphasizing "care of the soul" rather than just "curing" problems. His approach focuses on soulful living, re-enchantment of everyday life, and understanding how symptoms may be the soul calling for attention.

#### Dr. Jonice Webb - Childhood Emotional Neglect
Dr. Jonice Webb specializes in recognizing and healing Childhood Emotional Neglect (CEN), where emotional needs are unmet during formative years. Her work helps individuals develop self-awareness, recognize emotional suppression patterns, and develop healthier ways of meeting emotional needs.

#### Dr. Brené Brown - Shame, Empathy, Leadership
Dr. Brené Brown is a researcher and author who studies vulnerability, shame, and empathy. Her groundbreaking work shows that vulnerability is not weakness, but the birthplace of courage, creativity, and connection. She teaches that empathy is the antidote to shame and that wholehearted living leads to more authentic and fulfilling relationships and leadership.

#### Caroline Myss - Mysticism and Healing
Caroline Myss is a mystic and healer who studies the connection between spirituality and physical healing. Her work focuses on archetypes, energy medicine, and the soul's role in health and wellness.

#### Surya Das - Tibetan Buddhist Teachings
Surya Das is a Western teacher of Tibetan Buddhism, offering practical applications of Buddhist meditation and mindfulness for Western seekers. His teachings emphasize compassion, wisdom, and the integration of spiritual practice into daily life.

#### Chogyam Trungpa - Tibetan Buddhist Philosophy
Chogyam Trungpa was a Tibetan Buddhist meditation master who brought Tibetan Buddhism to the West. His teachings focus on mindfulness, meditation, and the integration of Buddhist principles with Western psychological understanding.

#### Pema Chodron - Buddhist Teacher
Pema Chödrön is a prominent American Buddhist nun who teaches the teachings of Shambhala Buddhism. Her focus is on developing compassion, embracing discomfort, and finding wisdom in challenging situations.

#### Jesse Cole - Banana Ball Founder
Jesse Cole is known for his innovative leadership approach with the Banana Ball Organization, which reverses traditional power dynamics in sports and education. His philosophy emphasizes joy, creativity, and service over competition and achievement.

#### Dr. David Burns - Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)
**Primary Texts**: "Feeling Good: The New Mood Therapy" (1980/2012), "Feeling Great: The Revolutionary New Treatment for Depression and Anxiety" (2020), "The Feeling Good Handbook" (1989/1999), "When Panic Attacks" (2006)

**Core Framework**: TEAM-CBT (Testing, Empathy, Agenda Setting, Methods) - evidence-based, medication-free approach to Major Depressive Disorder and anxiety

**Key Principles**:
- Thoughts create feelings (not events themselves)
- Depression/anxiety stem from 10 predictable cognitive distortions
- Systematic cognitive restructuring changes mood
- Daily Mood Log = primary tool (situation → emotions → thoughts → distortions → rational response → re-rate)
- Positive reframing BEFORE challenging (reduces resistance)
- Agenda setting addresses resistance before techniques
- Relapse prevention via personal toolbox + early warning system

**10 Cognitive Distortions**:
1. All-or-Nothing Thinking
2. Overgeneralization
3. Mental Filter
4. Disqualifying the Positive
5. Jumping to Conclusions (Mind Reading, Fortune Telling)
6. Magnification/Minimization
7. Emotional Reasoning
8. Should Statements
9. Labeling
10. Personalization

**Practical Applications**:
- **Daily Mood Log** - Core practice for real-time cognitive restructuring
- **Pleasure-Predicting Sheet** - Combats anhedonia via behavioral activation
- **Anti-Procrastination Sheet** - Breaks tasks into micro-steps with prediction/actual tracking
- **Fear Hierarchy + Exposure** - Systematic desensitization for anxiety/avoidance
- **Relationship Journal + EAR** - Empathy, Assertiveness, Respect for interpersonal conflicts
- **Core Belief Work** - Downward Arrow → Continuum → Evidence gathering
- **Externalization of Voices** - Role-play internal critic vs. compassionate responder
- **Magic Button / Miracle Question** - Assess readiness, clarify values

**Related Sources**: Stoicism (cognitive distancing), Buddhism (thoughts ≠ facts), Takeuchi (Behavioral Calibration × Pattern Recognition axis)

### Theological Sources

#### Unitarian Universalist Church
Unitarian Universalism is a modern liberal religious tradition that promotes freedom of religious thought and personal spiritual growth. It's characterized by its seven Principles and six Sources of authority. The theology emphasizes:
- **The inherent worth and dignity of every person**
- **Justice, equity, and compassion in human relations** 
- **World community of religions** - Respect for all faith traditions
- **A free and responsible search for truth and meaning**
- **Respect for the interdependent web of all existence**
- **Love and respect for nature**
- **Left-handed working toward a peaceful earth community**

This approach provides a framework for:
- Spiritual exploration without dogma
- Ethical living based on universal principles
- Interfaith dialogue and understanding
- Social justice activism
- Nature-centered spirituality
- Humanistic approach to meaning

#### Dr. John Spong - Modern Episcopal Theologian
Dr. John Spong is a progressive Episcopal theologian who challenges traditional Christian beliefs. His theology emphasizes:
- **Rejection of literal biblical interpretation** - Scripture as human document
- **Rational faith** - Faith that aligns with reason and evidence
- **Universalist tendencies** - God's love extends to all humanity
- **Modern relevance** - Ancient faith messages for contemporary questions
- **Interfaith dialogue** - Finding common ground across religions
- **Practical spirituality** - Religion as service, not worship

#### Druidry (Reformed Druids of North America)
Druidry is a modern Neoplatonic spiritual tradition combining ancient Celtic wisdom with contemporary American spirituality. It features:
- **Four Core Principles**: Reformed, Druid, North America, America
- **Three Primary Practices**: Ceremonies, Eisteddfod, Gwersu (work service)
- **Three Golden Rules**: Do no harm, love your work, take only what you need
- **Nature-centered spirituality** emphasizing environmental stewardship
- **Education through experience** with semester-long practical coursework

#### Shintoism
Ancient Japanese religion focusing on:
- **Concepts of purity** and ritual preparation
- **Animistic beliefs** - spirits in nature (kami)
- **Ritual practices** for maintaining harmony
- **Respect for tradition** while adapting to modern life
- **Prayer and worship** practices for spiritual connection
