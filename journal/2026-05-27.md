### Blog Post Draft

#### Can I get that in writing?

For some reason, I've always been interested in typography, fonts, writing stayles, etc. I really saw this play out while writing my dissertation. I didn't like the Word template supplied by the school. I still don't to be honest. So I was always looking for a better option. I know that TeX and LaTeX exist, but they can be very Byzantine at best. 

I explored and use some alternatives:

* [Quarto](https://quarto.org) - I used this for my website and this blog.
* [Typst](https://typst.app) - I'm interested in it and it's supported in Quarto, so I have looked into it.
* [Quarkdown](https://quarkdown.com/) - This is a newer kid on the block and looks neat.
* [Pandoc](https://pandoc.org) - The ultimate form converter.
* [Panache for Quarto](https://github.com/jolars/panache) - Interesting that Quarto already has someone building an alternative engine.
* [ConTeXt](https://wiki.contextgarden.net/Main_Page) - A more modern update of TeX
* [TypeTeX](https://www.typetex.app/) - More of a converter to encourage the switch to Typst.
* [mdv](https://github.com/drasimwagan/mdv) - This is the newest one that I've seen which is fascinating to read about.
