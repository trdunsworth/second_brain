#### AI in the Centre

Following from a LinkedIn post by MCP's CEO John Chiaramonte and their newest Steeped In post, I think that they've identified an issue that they've helped perpetuate. We're pushing centres to address personnel, volume, retention, and mental health issues by finding the right technological solutions to make things easier through the adoption of AI-enhanced tools. However, outside of a few outliers, most centre managers aren't technology experts. Smaller centres have more limited access to IT professionals who can make solid recommendations. Yes, companies like MCP or mine can consult with centres to make informed choices. However, we come at a cost too. If your budget for new technology is already small, it can be consumed by hiring an expert to give advice. Otherwise, centre managers have to listen to the vendors' promises and trust that they won't be steered wrong. I don't believe that any vendor would knowingly betray that trust and sell them a bill of goods.
