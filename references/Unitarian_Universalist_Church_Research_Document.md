# Theological Sources Research Documents

## Unitarian Universalist Church - Research Document

### Subject Name & Credentials

**Unitarian Universalist Church** - Modern liberal religious tradition with roots in the 19th century American religious landscape. UU promotes freedom of religious thought and personal spiritual growth. The denomination is characterized by its seven Principles and six Sources of belief.

### Core Philosophy/Teachings

#### The Seven Principles

1. **The inherent worth and dignity of every person** - Each individual possesses intrinsic value
2. **Justice, equity, and compassion in human relations** - Ethical treatment of all people
3. **World community of religions** - Respect for all faith traditions and spiritual paths
4. **A free and responsible search for truth and meaning** - Personal responsibility in spiritual exploration
5. **Respect for the interdependent web of all existence** - Recognition of ecological and social interconnectedness
6. **Love and respect for nature** - Spiritual connection with the natural world
7. **Left-handed working toward a peaceful earth community** - Commitment to world peace and human welfare

#### The Six Sources of Authority

1. **Scriptures** - Study of sacred texts from various religions
2. **Experience** - Personal spiritual experience and intuition
3. **Nature** - Wisdom found in observing the natural world
4. **Reason** - Rational understanding and critical thinking
5. **Humanistic teachings** - Ethical wisdom from human experience
6. **Spiritual teachings** - Universal spiritual insights across traditions

### Key Works/Contributions

- **"The Unitarian Universalist Witness"** - Historical overview of denominational development
- **"A New Christianity"** (Dr. John Spong) - Progressive theological re-imagining
- **"The Power of (and Without) God's Love"** - Contemporary UU theological reflection
- **"UUA Volunteer Ministry** - Practical service and outreach initiatives
