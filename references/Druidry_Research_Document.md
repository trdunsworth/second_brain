# Druidry (Reformed Druids of North America) - Research Document

## Subject Name & Credentials

**Druidry (Reformed Druids of North America - RDNA)** - Modern Neoplatonic spiritual tradition that emerged from practical coursework requirements at Carleton College in 1960. Founded by Dr. Theodore Roscoe and Harper Nightingale, the RDNA began as a way to fulfill college requirements through spiritual exploration and nature study. It's a North American adaptation of ancient Celtic Druid practices, characterized by its educational focus, non-theistic approach, and commitment to personal growth through nature connection.

## Core Philosophy/Teachings

### RDNA Tenets

Druidry follows four core principles outlined in the RDNA Constitution:

1. **Reformed** - Emphasis on reformation and personal spiritual development
2. **Druid** - Connection to ancient Celtic wisdom while adapting to modern context
3. **North America** - Localization of Celtic traditions to New World context
4. **America** - Democratic approach to spiritual practice and decision-making

### Three Primary Practices

1. **Ceremonies** - Seasonal celebrations and ritual work
2. **Eisteddfod** - Intellectual and social gatherings with poetry, music, and learning
3. **Gwersu** - Work-based learning and service to the community

### Core Philosophical Concepts

- **Nature as Sacred** - The natural world as source of spiritual wisdom
- **Personal Responsibility** - Self-guided spiritual development without hierarchical structure
- **Education through Experience** - Learning through direct engagement with nature and ritual
- **Community Building** - Building spiritual community through shared practices
- **Year Cycle Observation** - Following seasonal cycles for spiritual rhythm
- **Celtic Heritage Integration** - Connecting to ancient European spiritual roots

### Druid Ethics (The Three Golden Rules)

1. **Do no harm** - Non-violent approach to all actions
2. **Love the work you do** - Dedication and passion in service
3. **Take only what you need, give what you can** - Sustainable living and generosity

## Practical Implementation Steps

### Daily RDNA Practice (15-20 minutes)

#### 1. Nature Connection Meditation (5 minutes)

- Quiet observation of local environment
- Identify three things you've never noticed before
- Ask: "What is this place teaching me?"
- Respond with gratitude and presence

#### 2. Seasonal Awareness Journal (5 minutes)

- Note current seasonal changes
- Reflect on how they influence personal state
- Record what seasonal activities make sense
- Plan seasonal-appropriate practices

#### 3. Gwersu (Service) Practice (5 minutes)

- Identify one simple service you can provide today
- Consider what you can give without expectation of reward
- Practice work with dedication and love
- End with gratitude for the opportunity to serve

#### 4. Community Connection Check (3 minutes)

- Reach out to someone in your spiritual or social community
- Share something personal and meaningful
- Listen deeply to their experiences
- Practice building authentic connections
